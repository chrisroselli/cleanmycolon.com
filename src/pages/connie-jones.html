Experience Counts

Having grown up in North Carolina with a father who was a Family Medical Doctor, healthcare comes naturally to me. I was introduced to Colon Hydrotherapy in 1976, when confronting a 2-year diet pill addiction while speeding through a stressful financial career in New York City. My direct exposure to both conventional medicines and alternatives means I draw on a deeper understanding of your journey.

Throughout my career I have been committed to advancing both public and medical acceptance of this modality. Prior to the formation of I-ACT, where I served on the Board of Directors, I initiated regional professional organizations for colon therapists. In Boston, I founded and directed the New England School of Colon Hydrotherapy and for 24 years, I managed Constance Jones and Associates, which administered to over 50 clients a week. In Connecticut, I practiced at the Glastonbury Naturopathic Center for 11 years. I am excited about my new location at the Center for Progressive Therapies in Manchester and my affiliation with Dr. Jonathan Ritz, ND.

I helped pass the Public Act 13-305 (Compliance Issues), which became a CT law in 2013, allowing us to offer Colon Hydrotherapy with the referral of a Naturopath.

Another passion of mine is facilitating women’s rituals: including the Sacred Soul Sisters and Adult Girls into Women. I also assist my husband, Dr. Paul Dunion, as a facilitator in his Croton Mystery School. I am an animal lover who takes pleasure in spending time with our golden retriever, Bo.
